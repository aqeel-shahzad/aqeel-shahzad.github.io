About me

My route into analytics started with real business problems.

Before analytics became my degree, it was already part of how I approached work.

At Tes Global, I worked in commercial and client-facing sales across EMEA, where pipeline, outreach, market fit and stakeholder conversations were part of the job every day. I was recognised as a top-performing SDR for FY24 by number of opportunities created, but the part that shaped my direction was how much I relied on data to improve the way I worked.

I built dashboards for the EMEA region to understand lead sources, opportunity performance and success rates. I also mapped schools using market signals such as fee structure, region and fit, helping make outreach more focused and practical.

That experience showed me that analytics is not just about charts, code or models. It is about deciding where effort should go, what is working, and what action should come next.

Current dissertation project

Selected to work with MindWise on decision-focused analytics for service prioritisation.

Currently one of five students selected to work with MindWise on a dissertation project exploring how analytics can support fairer and more effective service allocation when resources are limited.

The project builds on a decision-focused analytics approach: instead of judging models only by prediction accuracy, it evaluates whether model outputs lead to better allocation decisions. The workflow links prediction models with knapsack optimisation, decision regret analysis, and fairness-aware constraints to understand which service requests should be prioritised under staff or time capacity limits.

The project is grounded in a real mental-health context: MindWise is a leading Northern Ireland mental health charity and a founding member of Mental Health UK.
